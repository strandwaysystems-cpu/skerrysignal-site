## 1. Positioning

### 1.1 The one-liner
**Skerry Signal builds and ranks lead generation websites in Atlantic Canada, then sends the inbound homeowner calls exclusively to one trade business per city.**

### 1.2 Who we serve (and who we don't)
- **In:** Owner-operator and small-team **HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical, and industrial supply** businesses, 1–15 employees, in **Atlantic Canada** (Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, PEI, Newfoundland & Labrador).
- **Out:** Creators, coaches, influencers, fitness trainers — that's **Smidstad's** lane. Also out: enterprise/national accounts, multi-location franchises, anyone outside Atlantic Canada.

### 1.3 The ideal client profile
- Owner-operator or GM is the decision-maker (no committee, no procurement).
- 1–15 employees.
- Already has demand but inconsistent lead flow ("some months booked, some months dead").
- Either has no website, a weak website, or is buying overpriced HomeStars / Google Ads leads.
- Has capacity to take 5–20 more booked jobs per month.
- Reachable by phone during business hours.

### 1.4 Why local trades are the right beachhead
- **23.6% of Canadian electricians and 18.8% of plumbers have no website at all** — the demand is literally invisible to Google but it's there.
- **Owner = decision-maker** → no committee, no purchasing department, no 90-day sales cycle.
- **The ROI math is immediate** — one $5,000 furnace install pays for months of fees.
- **Atlantic Canada is under-served vs. major metros** → less competition, faster time-to-rank, local-operator credibility.
- **Recurring revenue from day one** — pay-per-lead or retainer, not one-off projects.

### 1.5 The positioning wedge (what makes us different)
- **Exclusivity:** one trade business per city, period. Never two competing contractors on the same site.
- **Atlantic Canada operator:** we live here, we know the cities, we're not a Toronto agency cold-emailing east.
- **Vetted leads, not raw form fills:** every lead is AI-pre-qualified by Bland.ai before it hits the client's phone. A/B/C graded with recording attached.
- **No long contracts:** 60-day initial, month-to-month after. The leads have to perform or the client walks.
- **Speed of response:** under 30 minutes during business hours. Most agencies take days.

| **Core** ⭐ | **Retainer — Exclusive City** | **$1,500–$2,500 / month** | Trade owners ready to commit, want predictable cost | Everything in PPL + **city exclusivity** + monthly performance report + priority response + included call tracking + GBP optimization | 60-day initial, month-to-month thereafter |
| **Premium** | **Multi-Trade or Multi-City Retainer** | **$3,500–$6,000+ / month** | Growing operators with multiple service lines or service cities | Multiple lead gen assets working in parallel, dedicated weekly review call, custom qualification rules, full marketing-ops layer | 90-day initial, quarterly review |

### 2.1 Client #1 pricing posture (read this)
**Client #1 is deliberately priced below market.** The goal of the first close is a signed contract, a case study, and a testimonial — not maximum revenue. Discount PPL to $50–75/lead or retainer to $1,000–1,500/mo for the founding client only. **Client #2 onward gets full pricing** because the case study is now leverage worth 1.5–2× reply rate on every subsequent campaign.

### 2.2 What counts as a billable lead (write this into the contract)
- **A-grade (80–100):** Homeowner, in service area, ready this week, big-ticket or relevant service. Billable.
- **B-grade (60–79):** Homeowner, in service area, relevant service, slightly longer timeline. Billable.
- **C-grade (40–59):** Delivered for awareness but **not billable** without client opt-in.
- **Junk (0–39):** Out of area / tenant / wrong service / spam. Never delivered, never billed.

### 2.3 Add-ons (the margin layer)

- **Retainer:** Monthly upfront, Stripe subscription. First month + setup billed at contract signature.

## 4. The 90-Day Outreach + Close Calendar

Trade lead-gen sales cycles are weeks, not days. This calendar runs three parallel tracks: **outreach pipeline, asset performance, client delivery.**

| Day(s) | Track | Action | Goal / Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Day 1** | Setup | Domain registered, branded email created, **warmup started** | Day 22 = earliest outreach date |
| **Days 1–21** | Setup | Build agency wrapper §3.1, lead gen asset §3.3, qualification stack §3.4, tool stack §3.5 — **all in parallel with warmup** | Sales machine fully assembled by Day 21 |
| **Days 1–21** | Asset | HVAC Halifax site indexed, GBP submitted, citations growing, GSC live | Ranking signals forming (map pack often appears first) |
| **Day 18–20** | Outreach prep | Apollo list build: 500–800 verified HVAC owner contacts in Halifax + Atlantic Canada metros. Clay enrichment running | List ready to load into Instantly |
| **Day 21** | Outreach prep | LinkedIn outreach starts (50 connection requests/week, ramping) — does not need email warmup | Early signal, accepted connections, replies |
| **Day 22** | **Outreach launch** | **Email Wave 1:** Instantly sends Email 1 to first 50 contacts. Loom embed live | First opens & replies begin |
| **Day 25** | Outreach | **Email 2** (Loom video) sends to non-responders | Reply rate spike — Loom is the highest-converting element |
| **Day 29** | Outreach | **Bland.ai cold calling activates** on email non-responders (100–150 dials/week) | Voice replies + voicemails captured |
| **Day 29** | Outreach | **Email 3** (case logic, numbers) sends | Late-funnel closes |
| **Days 22–35** | Reply handling | Every reply → response within 30 min during business hours → book Calendly discovery call | 15–30% of replies → booked calls |
| **Day 30+** | Outreach | Ramp to **150 emails/day, 30 LinkedIn/day, 30 calls/day**. Multiple inboxes if scaling past 200/day | Steady pipeline |
| **Day 34** | Outreach | **Email 4** (permission close) — "should I close the file?" | Recovers ~10% of cold leads |
| **Days 30–60** | Closing | Discovery calls held → 1-page proposals sent within 1 hour → contracts out → **first close** | Realistic first-client window: **Day 35–60** |
| **Days 35–90** | Delivery | Onboard client #1: re-route CallRail to client's phone, update GBP description, update Bland.ai script to mention client by name | Client receiving leads within 24h of signature |
| **Day 7 post-close** | Delivery | First weekly client report sent. First-week invoice issued (PPL) or first month confirmed (retainer) | Trust + cash flow established |
| **Day 30 post-close** | Flywheel | 20-min review call: show leads delivered, jobs booked, revenue delivered. **Capture testimonial (written + video).** Ask for referral | Case study unlocked → next campaign reply rates 1.5–2× |
| **Days 60–90** | Scale | Run `case-study-builder` skill. Open Campaign #2 (HVAC Moncton OR plumbing Halifax — whichever the `vertical-expansion-evaluator` favors). Loop §3.3 for the new asset | Second lead gen site live, second campaign launching |

### 4.1 Daily rhythm during active outreach
- **30 new emails sent** (Instantly auto-handles within sending cap)
- **10 LinkedIn connection requests + DMs to acceptances**
- **20 Bland.ai dials** (parallel to email, on Day 7+ of each contact's sequence)
- **Reply within 30 min** to every positive/curious response — speed of reply correlates more with close rate than any other variable
- **15 min weekly review** of metrics: open rate, reply rate, calls booked, deals closed
- **Estimate:** 60–90 min/day of active human work; the rest is automation

### 4.2 Expected funnel math (per campaign of 500–800 contacts)
- **Open rate:** 25–45% (warmed domain)
- **Positive reply rate:** 3–5% target, 8–12% on top-quartile campaigns → ~15–40 positive replies
- **Reply → booked call:** 15–30% → **5–10 discovery calls**
- **Call → close:** 15–25% for trade leadgen → **1–3 signed clients per campaign**

> **If reply rate < 2% by Day 14:** diagnose deliverability first (open rate < 20% = re-warmup), then list quality, then messaging. Pause and fix; don't burn the domain.

### 5.6 Discovery call script (15 minutes, on Calendly)

**0:00–2:00 · Rapport + context**
> "Thanks for hopping on. Before I get into specifics, tell me about [business] — how long have you been at it, team size, the kind of work you take on?"

Listen for: capacity (slow/full/booked), preferred job types, ticket sizes, current marketing spend.

**2:00–5:00 · Pain validation**
> - "Where are your leads coming from today?"
> - "If you had 5 more booked jobs next month, what would that look like for the business?"
> - "What hasn't worked when you've tried marketing in the past?"

**5:00–10:00 · Solution walkthrough**
> Share Loom screen: site ranking → CallRail call log → Airtable lead delivery format with AI qualification → Bland.ai call recording.
>
> Then: **"Only one HVAC company per city gets these leads. That's the deal."**

**10:00–12:00 · Pricing**
> Two options, then stop talking:
> - **Pay-per-lead:** $[X] per qualified call. Only A and B grades billable. Weekly invoicing.
> - **Flat monthly retainer:** $[X]/month, exclusive, 60-day initial then month-to-month.

**12:00–14:00 · Objection handling**
> - *"I need to think about it"* → "Totally fair — what specifically would you want to think through? Anything I can answer now?"
> - *"Too expensive"* → "Walk me through a typical install ticket — let's see what one job covers."
> - *"What if it doesn't work?"* → "First 30 days no-contract — if you don't book a job from the leads, you don't pay. Fair?"
> - *"How is this different from HomeStars / Google Ads?"* → "Exclusivity. They sell the same lead to 5 competitors. We sell to one company per city, period."

**14:00–15:00 · Close**
> "Sounds like a fit. Want me to send the agreement over? You'll have it in your inbox within the hour."
>
> Get verbal yes. End call.

## 8. The Flywheel (After Client #1)

## 11. Hard Rules (Don't Break These)

1. **No two contractors in the same vertical + city.** Exclusivity is the entire moat. Two contractors = lose both within 90 days when they discover each other.
2. **The 21-day warmup is non-negotiable.** Sending cold mail from a fresh domain torches the brand permanently.
3. **Speed of reply during business hours: under 30 minutes.** This single variable correlates more with close rate than any other.
4. **Don't run more than one campaign per branded inbox simultaneously.** Use multiple inboxes if scaling past 200/day.
5. **Always send the Loom.** Text-only emails get half the reply rate. The Loom is the proof; never run a campaign without it.
6. **A and B-grade leads are billable, C is not.** Defined in the contract, enforced by Bland.ai recording. No dispute survives the recording.
7. **First-client pricing is below market.** Don't try to maximize on client #1. The case study is worth more than the dollar delta.
8. **Skerry Signal stays in trades. Smidstad stays in creators.** Brand discipline keeps positioning sharp and outreach copy specific.
9. **Document every campaign in Airtable.** What you can't measure, you can't iterate.
10. **Recordings on every Bland.ai call.** Legal protection + dispute resolution + qualification tuning material.
